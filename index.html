Sacred Destinations

Discover the divine essence of Pushkar through its temples and sacred sites

Sacred Lake

Pushkar Lake

One of India's most sacred lakes, surrounded by 52 bathing ghats and over 500 temples. The lake is believed to have been created by Lord Brahma's lotus petals.

Ancient Temple

Brahma Temple

Built in the 14th century, this temple is one of the few dedicated to Lord Brahma worldwide.

Hilltop Temple

Savitri Temple

Dedicated to Brahma's first wife Savitri, this temple sits atop Ratnagiri Hill, requiring a challenging trek or cable car ride.

Cultural Event

Pushkar Camel Fair

One of the largest and most vibrant livestock fairs in the world, the Pushkar Camel Fair draws thousands of visitors every year.

South Indian Style

Rangji Temple

A unique architectural marvel combining South Indian, Rajasthani, and Mughal styles.

Market

Pushkar Bazaar

A vibrant marketplace famous for textiles, jewelry, and handicrafts.

Ancient Temple

Varaha Temple

One of Pushkar's oldest temples, dedicated to Varaha, Vishnu's boar incarnation.

Heritage Palace

Man Mahal

A stunning palace-turned-heritage hotel built by Raja Man Singh I.

Hill Temple

Pap Mochani Temple

Situated on a hilltop, this temple is believed to absolve devotees of their sins.

Historic Temple

Old Rangji Temple

The original Rangji temple showcases pure Rajasthani architecture with intricate stone carvings and traditional paintings.

Heritage Site

Pushkar Fort

A historic fortification offering panoramic views of Pushkar.

Sacred Temple

Gayatri Temple

Dedicated to Goddess Gayatri, this serene temple features traditional architecture and beautiful gardens.

Scenic Spot

Sunset Point

A picturesque viewpoint offering breathtaking views of Pushkar Lake and the Aravalli range.

Nature

Rose Garden

Pushkar's famous rose gardens produce world-renowned flowers used in perfumes and attars.

Sacred Sites

More Sacred Sites

Explore Atmateshwar Temple's ancient Shiva lingam, Sri Sankat Mochan Hanuman Temple's vibrant ceremonies, Shree Shyam Temple's beautiful architecture, and Saraswati Temple's peaceful surroundings.

About Pushkar

Pushkar, a serene town in Rajasthan, is renowned for its sacred Pushkar Lake and the world-famous Brahma Temple, one of the few dedicated to Lord Brahma. Known as the "Rose Garden of Rajasthan," Pushkar captivates visitors with its vibrant culture, ancient temples, and bustling bazaars.

The annual Pushkar Camel Fair attracts thousands with its unique blend of tradition and festivity. Visitors can enjoy breathtaking sunsets from Savitri Temple and explore the charm of Pushkar's quaint streets.

A haven for spiritual seekers and cultural enthusiasts, Pushkar offers a harmonious blend of history, devotion, and natural beauty, making it a must-visit destination.
